Interbranch Transfer Entry Table

The line item table contains the following columns:

Quantity - The quantity of the part that you are transferring from one warehouse to another.

Part ID - The part that you are transferring from one warehouse to another.

Quantity Shipped - The quantity of parts that you successfully ship to another warehouse.

Quantity Received - The quantity of parts that you successfully receive in the receiving warehouse.

Transit Location ID - The warehouse location from which you are shipping the parts. You must define a warehouse location as a transit location before it appears in the Transit Location ID list box. You can enter a unique ID on the fly and VISUAL asks if you want to add this warehouse as a transit location. This is a required field.

U/M - The unit of measure of the part.

Unit Price - The price per unit of the part.

Extension - The price per unit of the part multiplied by the quantity of parts.

Text - Text regarding the part or the transfer in general. You enter this information in the Line Specs note box.

Specs - VISUAL selects this box if there is any text regarding the IBT line.

Demand Quantity Allocated - The quantity of the IBT that you have allocated to demand.

Demand Quantity Fulfilled - The demand quantity that has been fulfilled from by the supplying source.

Supply Quantity Allocated - The quantity that you have allocated as supply to the IBT line

Supply Quantity Fulfilled - The supply quantity that has been fulfilled by the supplying source.

Cartons - The number of cartons you use in the shipment.

Shipping Weight - The weight of the shipment.

Weight U/M - The unit of measure that you use to weigh the part that you are transferring.

Package Type - The type of package you want to use to ship the IBT. Enter packaging types in Application Global Maintenance.
