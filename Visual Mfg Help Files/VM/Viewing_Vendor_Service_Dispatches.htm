Viewing Vendor Service Dispatches

The Vendor Service Dispatch window allow you to view the status of parts sent to an outside service vendor.

f you are licensed to use multiple sites, you can view service dispatches on a site-by-site basis.

  1. If you are licensed to use multiple sites, click the Site ID arrow and select the site to view. If you are licensed to use a single site, this field is unavailable.

  2. Select Vendor Service Dispatches from the Info menu.

The Vendor Service Dispatches window appears.

  1. Enter the Vendor ID in the Vendor ID field.

  2. If you want to exclude fully received orders, select the Exclude Fully Received check box.

You can view the following information for each line.

Service - The service the vendor provides.

Vendor Service ID - The service vendor ID.

Service Part ID - The part ID for the part the vendor services.

Purchase Order - The Purchase Order ID for the service.

Purchase Line # - The purchase line number for the service.

Order Quantity - The order quantity for a purchase order of operation start quantity when dispatched against an operation with no associated purchase order.

Order Date - The order date from the purchase order or the suggested dispatch date for an operation without a purchase order.

P/O Desired Receive Date - The desired receive date of the purchase order

Promise Date - The date the parts will be returned from a purchase order of service required date for an operation without a purchase order.

Dispatch ID - The dispatch ID.

Dispatch Line # - The dispatch line number.

User Dispatch Quantity - The part amount sent to the outside service vendor in the vendor’s unit of measure.

Dispatched Quantity - The part amount dispatched in your unit of measure.

Dispatch Date - The date the parts were dispatched.

Dispatch Desired Receive Date The date you want the serviced parts returned.

Unit Price - The price per unit.

Discount % - The service discount percent.

Base Charge - The one time fixed cost for performing the basic service. This cost plus the cost per unit multiplied by the units of service is the total cost for a specific service.

Minimum Charge - The minimum that the system will charge for a service. If the service ordered costs more than the minimum charge allowed, then the system charges the actual cost. If the service ordered cost is less than the minimum charge, the system charges the minimum charge.

Standard Unit Cost - The part cost from Part Maintenance.

Linked Operations - The work order for which the service is performed.

You can sort the Service Dispatch History table by Dispatch Date, Expected Receive Date, Dispatch ID, Order Date, Purchase Order.

You can sequence any sort in Ascending or Descending order.
