Material Netting

The Material Planning Window displays basic planning information about a part and performs material netting. Material Netting is a process of comparing demand and supply at given points, and projecting a net balance and possible exceptions. An exception is a condition that may require an action by you, such as a stock out, and may also be phrased as a suggestion of action to take.

There are four classes of information that appear in the window:

Part Information - The Material Planning Window works with one part at a time. The top section shows all of the basic material planning and availability information for the selected part. This information cannot be modified.

Demand Information - The left-hand columns of the table show information on demand orders. A demand order is either a work order or a customer order line that causes a requirement for the current part. Note that when a demand order is a work order, the demand is actually caused by a specific operation of the work order. If multiple operations require the same material, each one is a separate requirement.

Supply Information - The right hand columns of the table show information on supply orders. A supply order is a purchase order or work order that produces a supply of the part.

Netting Information - Supply and demand orders are displayed and aligned based on the netting options selected (see below). The Projected Quantity, Exception, and Suggested Release columns then display the net quantity on-hand, any exception message produced by VISUAL based on this quantity, and a suggested release date for a new supply order.

The Material Planning Window table is configurable. There are many columns in this table. You may especially wish to hide, move, resize, or rename some of the columns in this table to make viewing easier.
