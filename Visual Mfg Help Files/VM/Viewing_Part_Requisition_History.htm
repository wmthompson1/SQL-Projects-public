Viewing Part Requisition History

The Part Requisition History feature allows you to view a part’s requisition history. A part’s complete requisition history, including order dates, order IDs, and linked purchase order information, is available.

  1. If you are licensed to use multiple sites, click the Site ID arrow and select the site to view in the history dialog box. If you are licensed to use a single site, this field is unavailable.

  2. To view a part requisition history, highlight a requisition line and click the Requisition History button on the Table toolbar or select Line Requisition History from the Info menu.

The Part Requisition History dialog box appears, displaying the Part ID on the requisition line.

  1. If the dialog box is empty, click the Part ID button to choose the part for which you want to view a requisition history.

VISUAL displays the part’s history, an order per line.

  1. Use the option buttons in the Sequence section to sort in ascending or descending order. Use the options in the Sort section to sort by order, date, desired receive date, or purchase order.

Use both to configure more focused presentation of information.

Requisition Date - The initiation date of the purchase requisition.

Desired Recv Date - The desired receive date of the purchase requisition.

Requisition ID - The Purchase Requisition ID containing the part.

Delivery Schedule - If selected, the requisition contains a delivery schedule.

Vendor - The vendor from the requisition header.

Order Quantity - The part requisition quantity.

Currency - The currency from the requisition.

Unit Price - The price per unit of the requisitioned part.

Native Currency - The native currency of the vendor.

Native Unit Price - The price per unit in the native currency.

Discount % - The requisition discount percent, if any.

Fixed Cost - The one-time cost from the purchase requisition line.

Standard Unit Cost - The standard part cost from the part master.

  1. To view all aspects of a purchase requisition in Purchase Requisition Entry, select the line and click the View button or double-click the line.

  2. Click the Close button to return to the Purchase Requisition Entry window.
