What is the Scheduling Window?

The Scheduling Window lets you view the shop schedule relative to firmed work orders and work orders released to the floor. Its flexibility gives you the ability to view multiple resources and even multiple schedules at the same time. If you are using the Global Scheduler, this window is a must for viewing the results of the schedule.

Three major reasons for creating a schedule are to understand how much of your capacity is spoken for already, to identify possible future threats to on-time deliveries, and to help your production, procurement and sales departments to work together more successfully. Because both load and capacity are dimensions of a schedule, VISUAL provides the ability to have many different schedules. This allows you to simulate any number of load/capacity scenarios. The production schedule is treated as the real schedule for material planning and work dispatching purposes.

You may wish to consider a Windows program scheduler, which has the capability of running a program like the Concurrent Scheduler at a certain time without the system being attended. Some alternative desktop packages support this feature, as well as some automatic backup systems. You may wish to consider this for the Costing Utilities as well.
