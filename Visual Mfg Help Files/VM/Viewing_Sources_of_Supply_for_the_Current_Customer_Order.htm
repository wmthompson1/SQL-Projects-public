Viewing Sources of Supply for the Current Customer Order

After you have allocated supply to a customer order, you can view from where the supply is coming. For example, supply may come directly from inventory; it may be coming in the form a purchase order that you have not yet received or from a work order that you have not yet finished.

Supply may come from these six sources:

  • Co-product supply from a work order (CP)

  • Inventory (I)

  • Purchase Orders (PO)

  • Purchase Order Delivery Schedule lines (PD)

  • Interbranch Transfers (WH)

  • Work Orders (WO)

  1. With an order open in VISUAL Order Management Customer Order Entry window, maximize the Order Management window and open the Current Customer Order window if it is not already open.

The Current Customer Order ID appears next to the icon in the left pane of the window.

  1. Double-click the icon.

All lines for the current customer order appear.

  1. Click the plus box next to the line for which you want to view supply allocation information.

For example, a customer order contains one line for 5,000 MMT-1996-C05 parts. In order to fulfill the demand of the order, supply has been assigned from all six potential supplying sources.

It is quite possible that you may use any combination of these previous six supply sources to meet an order’s supply requirements; it is also quite possible that you may have sufficient stock in inventory alone to fulfill an order.
