**Inventory Issuance**  
 *.vs. Usage*

**Issuance of inventory does not always equal the usage (or consumption) of inventory**. Issuance refers to the act of removing items from a main storage location, while usage is the total amount of inventory that has been consumed or is no longer in stock due to all possible reasons over a specific period. \[[1](https://www.netsuite.com/portal/resource/articles/inventory-management/inventory-usage.shtml#:~:text=Inventory%20usage%20is%20a%20comprehensive%20measure%20of,as%20through%20donations%2C%20theft%2C%20damage%2C%20and%20spoilage.), [2](https://www.netsuite.com/portal/resource/articles/inventory-management/inventory-usage.shtml), [3](https://www.netsuite.com/portal/resource/articles/inventory-management/inventory-usage.shtml#:~:text=Inventory%20usage%20is%20a%20direct%20measure%20of,reasons%2C%20such%20as%20theft%2C%20expiration%2C%20or%20damage.), [4](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6641805/#:~:text=The%20data%20also%20showed%20that%20inventory%20levels,have%20a%20consistent%20relationship%20to%20consumption%20volumes.)\]

Key Distinctions

| Feature \[[1](https://www.netsuite.com/portal/resource/articles/inventory-management/inventory-usage.shtml#:~:text=Inventory%20usage%20is%20a%20comprehensive%20measure%20of,as%20through%20donations%2C%20theft%2C%20damage%2C%20and%20spoilage.), [2](https://www.netsuite.com/portal/resource/articles/inventory-management/inventory-usage.shtml), [5](https://docs.oracle.com/en/applications/jd-edwards/supply-chain-manufacturing/9.2/eoaim/understanding-inventory-issues.html), [6](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/ayoubwaziri_different-between-issuing-and-dispatch-activity-7317596347660918790-qxPZ), [7](https://docs.ifs.com/maintenix/25r1/Operator/enduser/Supply_Chain/materials/demand_management/c_inventory_issue.html#:~:text=In%20the%20physical%20world%2C%20inventory%20is%20issued,and%20for%20other%20aspects%20of%20material%20control.)\] | Issuance of Inventory | Usage of Inventory |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Definition** | The formal process of releasing items from a storeroom or warehouse, typically for internal use, a specific job, or transfer to another location. | The total quantity of inventory that has gone from "in stock" to "not in stock" over a specified time frame. |
| **Scope** | A specific transaction or movement of goods recorded when they leave a central inventory point. | A broader metric that includes items issued for use, but also accounts for other losses such as sales, donations, theft, damage, spoilage, or obsolescence. |
| **Timing** | Recorded at the point of physical removal from storage. | Measured over a period of time, often using a formula (Starting Inventory \+ Received Inventory \- Ending Inventory). |

When They Might Differ

* **Items in transit or staging:** Inventory might be issued from the main warehouse but not yet "used" in the final application or consumed by the end-user; it could be in a staging area or in transit to another site.  
* **Shrinkage:** Usage calculations account for unexpected losses like breakage, theft, or items expiring, which might not be immediately recorded as a formal "issue" transaction in a system until a physical count reveals the discrepancy.  
* **Internal transfers vs. consumption:** An item issued from a central warehouse to a different internal department's smaller stockroom is an *issuance* but not yet final *usage* or consumption by the end process (e.g., in a manufacturing line). \[[1](https://www.netsuite.com/portal/resource/articles/inventory-management/inventory-usage.shtml#:~:text=Inventory%20usage%20is%20a%20comprehensive%20measure%20of,as%20through%20donations%2C%20theft%2C%20damage%2C%20and%20spoilage.), [2](https://www.netsuite.com/portal/resource/articles/inventory-management/inventory-usage.shtml), [6](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/ayoubwaziri_different-between-issuing-and-dispatch-activity-7317596347660918790-qxPZ), [8](https://quizlet.com/192536959/inventory-and-warehouse-management-processes-chapter-7-flash-cards/), [9](https://qcouriers.co.uk/2025/09/goods-in-transit-and-in-transit-inventory/#:~:text=Understanding%20Goods%20in%20Transit%20and%20Their%20Impact,inventory%20records%20and%20ensuring%20supply%20chain%20efficiency.)\]

In summary, issuance is a transactional step in the inventory management process, whereas usage is a broader measure of overall depletion from stock for all reasons.
