# Viewing Purchase Price History

In the Service Purchase History dialog box you can
view the following information for each line:

Service Part ID - The part ID you send
to the outside service vendor

Purchase Order - The Purchase Order ID
for that service.

Purchase Line Number - The purchase order
line number for that service.

Vendor - The vendor that supplies the
outside service.

Order Quantity - The total part amount
you send to the outside service vendor.

Received Quantity - The total serviced
part amount you have received from the outside service to date.

Dispatched Quantity - The part amount
you send to the outside service to date.

Currency - The currency you use to purchase
the outside service.

Unit Price - The price per unit.

Discount % - The service discount percent.

Base Charge - The one time fixed cost
for performing the basic service. This cost plus the cost per unit,
multiplied by the units of service, is the total cost for a specific
service.

Minimum Charge - The minimum VISUAL charges
for a service. If the service you order costs more than the minimum
charge allowed, VISUAL charges the actual cost. If the service you
order costs is less than the minimum charge, VISUAL charges the minimum
charge.

Standard Unit Cost - The part cost from
Part Maintenance

Linked Operations - The work order you
use operation to perform the operation.

You can sort the Service Purchase History table by Order Date, Desired
Recv Date, or Purchase Order, and sequence the table in Ascending
or Descending order.

Select a line and click the View button
to inspect a purchase order that corresponds to a line in the table.

The Purchase Order window appears with the selected purchase order.
You can view any other details of the Purchase Order that do not appear
in the table, including notations and specifications. If you are currently
editing or creating a Purchase Order, VISUAL prompts you to confirm
the new or changed information before the View option resets the window.
