# Viewing Available to Customer Part Quantities

Available to customer part quantity information is
one level down the Part Availability tree.

In the Part Availability window, double-click the icon in the left
pane of the window.

The structure expands to the next level.

Detail information appears in the right pane as follows:

Available to Customer - The quantity of
the part that is currently available to a customer. VISUAL determined
this value based on the customers priority and disbursement fill
rate, or the percent of the total available SKU quantity in a warehouse
to which a customer has access.

Available - The quantity that is currently
available to you to assign to a customer order. VISUAL determines
this value by subtracting the Committed quantity of the part from
the On Hand quantity of the part.

On Hand - The quantity of the part that
you have in a warehouse or warehouse locations regardless of availability.

Committed - The portion of the On Hand
quantity that is committed to customer orders, work order material
requirements, and interbranch transfers.

Expected - The quantity of parts that
you expect to receive from work orders, purchase orders, and interbranch
transfers.

Expected/Committed - The quantity of the
part that is both expected or committed through supply allocation
or demand allocation to customer orders, work order material requirements,
and interbranch transfers.

On Hold - The quantity of the part that
you have placed on hold. These parts exist in inventory but are not
available for order fulfillment. Place parts on hold in Warehouse
Maintenance.

Locked - The quantity of the part that
you have locked in inventory. These parts exist in inventory but are
not available for order fulfillment. Lock parts in inventory in Warehouse
Maintenance.

Unavailable - The quantity of the part
that is currently unavailable.

Minimum Inventory - The quantity of the
part that you must keep in inventory.

Unallocated Demand - The quantity of the
part that you have not allocated to demand.
