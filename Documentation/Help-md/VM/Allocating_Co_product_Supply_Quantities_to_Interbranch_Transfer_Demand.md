# Allocating Co-product Supply Quantities to Interbranch Transfer Demand

To allocate co-product supply to interbranch transfer
demand:

1. Select Eng/Mfg,
   Manufacturing Window.
2. Open the work order
   that contains the co-product.
3. Open the work order
   header card.
4. Click the Co-products
   button.
5. For the co-product that
   you are assigning to demand, specify the receiving warehouse in
   the Receiving Warehouse column. Click Save.
6. Select the co-product
   and click Assign to Demand. This information
   is displayed:

Work Order ID-
The ID of the work order that produces this coproduct.

Desired Qty -
The quantity of the coproduct that you want the work order to produce.
This is the total quantity you can allocate to all supply sources.

Received Qty -
The quantity of the coproduct Desired Quantity that you have received
into inventory. A work order must be released before you can receive
any coproduct quantities into inventory.

Allocated Qty
- The portion of the Desired Quantity that you have allocated thus
far to demand.

Fulfilled Qty
- The quantity that has been fulfilled from an allocation. When you
issue this quantity to the demand source, the demand allocation is
then fulfilled.

Available Qty
- The remaining quantity that is available to be allocated.

Part ID - The
ID of the co-product.

Part Description
- A description of the co-product.

Stock U/M - The
stock unit of measure of the co-product.

Warehouse ID -
The Warehouse ID of the coproduct.

Sched Finish Date
- The date on which the work order responsible for the production
of this co-product is scheduled to finish.

Desired Want Date
- The date by which you want to receive the co-product into inventory.

7. Click Insert.
8. In the Type column,
   select WH.
9. Double-click the Demand Base ID button.  A list of interbranch
   transfers with unallocated demand for the co-product is displayed.
10. Optionally, use the
    check boxes and date fields to filter the information in the table.
11. Select the interbranch
    transfer to which you are allocating supply.
12. Click Ok.
13. In the Allocate Quantity
    column, enter the co-product supply quantity that you are allocating
    to the interbranch transfer. You cannot enter a quantity that
    is greater than the Demand Unallocated Quantity of the interbranch
    transfer or the Available Quantity of the co-product.
14. Click the Save
    button.
