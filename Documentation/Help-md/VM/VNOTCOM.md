# Working with Notations

Although Notation windows look similar to specification
windows, they are quite different. Whereas specifications are one
piece of free-form text, notations are arranged into entries, each
one having a date and time stamp. When you use notations, you view,
modify, add, or delete an entry. Each entry has a number, which appears
in the upper right corner of the notation window.

## Viewing Notations

When you first call up a notation window, and after you save notations,
you are in view mode. The total number of notation entries appears
at the upper right. Notations are listed from the most recent entry
to the oldest, each preceded by its time stamp. You can scroll up
and down using the scroll bar to view all of the notations.

## Adding Notation Entries

To add a new notation:

1. Click the New
   button.

The notation window clears, and the current
date and time appear. This is the time stamp for the new notation.

2. Type any amount of text
   into the Notes window.

Use Windows cut, copy, and paste whenever
necessary.

3. Click the Save
   button to save the new entry.

## Modifying Notation Entries

To modify a notation, you must first navigate to the
entry you want to change. To move to the latest entry, press the double-up-arrow button. To move to the oldest
entry (entry number 1), press the double-down-arrow
button. To move back an entry, press the single
down-arrow button. To advance forward an entry, press the single up-arrow button.

When you reach the entry you want to modify, click the Edit
button. The window becomes editable, and you can make any necessary
changes. Click the Save button to save the
changes.

## Deleting Notation Entries

To delete an entry, navigate to it, then click the
Del button. The entry is marked deleted.
To commit all marked deletes, click the Save
button.
