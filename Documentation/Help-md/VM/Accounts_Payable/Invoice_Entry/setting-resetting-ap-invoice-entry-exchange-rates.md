# Setting/Resetting A/P Invoice Entry Exchange Rates

When working with foreign-currency invoices, exchange rates determine the local currency amounts posted to ledgers.

Guidance:

1. Set the invoice currency and confirm the effective exchange rate for the invoice date.
2. If exchange rates change before posting, decide whether to revalue the invoice or record variances per policy.
3. To reset the rate, edit the invoice exchange rate field (if permitted) and save; document the reason for the change.
4. During posting, ensure the system uses the correct rate and posts any realized/unrealized FX gains or losses per accounting rules.

Notes:

- Avoid ad-hoc rate changes without approval; maintain a table of daily rates or integrate with a rate service.
- Reconciliation: compare vendor invoice currency totals with expected local currency amounts when reconciling.
