# Spare Part and Repair Demand

Spare part or repair part demand is the result of
independent demand for these parts, even though they are planned for
production as part of a larger assembly. In this case the demand profile
is more or less upside down. A normal end item typically has more
independent demand than dependent demand. A spare part or repair part
typically has more dependent demand than independent demand.

Ultimately, the idea of processing forecasts is to end up with a
master schedule for each part being processed. Forecasts are converted
to master schedules by some mechanism that transfers, analyzes, or
calculates, usually with human intervention, the quantities of the
forecast into quantities in the master schedule. In some cases, forecasting
is broken down into to several separate forecasts. This division is
generally made on the cause of the demand. This same division can,
but does not have to be applied to the master schedule.

Demand might be caused by normal customer demand, repair part demand
or spare part demand. You can break these down for various reasons.
By offering multiple forecasts, VISUAL makes it possible for the user
to establish each individual forecast separately. All that remains
is a mechanism that allows the user to combine several forecasts to
obtain a master schedule.
