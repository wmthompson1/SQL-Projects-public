# Locking Records

Estimates, customer orders, purchase orders, and invoices
(receivables) are all records you send to customers or vendors. After
you send these records, altering them is generally inappropriate.
For example, a customer order is a contract between the two parties
involved, one selling a product and one buying it. It contains prices,
quantities, delivery dates, and specifications. After you issue a
customer order to the buying party, it is important that you can always
remember and retrieve it.

Records are locked after you print them. For estimates, quotes,
and A/R invoices, the record is locked after you print it. For customer
orders and purchase orders, the record is locked after you print order
acknowledgments.

The Print dialog box for each of these records includes a Draft
Mode check box. Select this check box to print a the record without
locking the record. When you print in draft mode, the word DRAFT
is shown in the header of the record.
