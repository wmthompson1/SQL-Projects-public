# Viewing On-Hold Part Information

On-hold part information is one level down the Part
Availability tree.

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| POSTIT.gif | Place parts on hold in Warehouse Maintenance. If you have placed a specific part on hold, the progress monitor next to On Hold appears full of red. This indicates that the progress for this task has gone past its normal threshold, meaning that with all parts on hold, you cannot presently use available inventory. When on hold, a parts availabe quantities appear as negative values. |

In the Part Availability window, double-click the icon in the left
pane of the window. The structure expands to the next level.

Highlight On Hold.

Detail information appears in the right pane as follows:

Available to Customer - The quantity of
the part that is currently available to a customer.

Available - The quantity that is currently
available to assign to a customer order. VISUAL determines this value
by subtracting the Committed quantity of
the part from the On Hand quantity of the
part.

On Hand - The quantity of the part that
you have in a warehouse or warehouse locations.

Committed - The portion of the On Hand
quantity that is committed to a customer order, work order material
requirement and interbranch transfer.

Expected - The quantity of parts that
you expect to receive from work orders, purchase orders, and interbranch
transfers.

Expected/Committed - The quantity of the
part that is expected or committed through supply allocation or demand
allocation of customer orders, work order material requirements, and
interbranch transfers.

On Hold - The quantity of the part that
is on hold.

Locked - The quantity of the part that
is locked.

Unavailable - The quantity of the part
that is unavailable to you to use for a customer order.

Minimum Inventory - The quantity of the
part that you must keep in inventory.

Unallocated Demand - The quantity of the
part that you have not allocated to demand.
