# Understanding Billing and Revenue Recognition

For Project and Aerospace and Defense industries,
VISUAL bases the journal entries associated against shipments on your
Estimates At Completion (EAC) instead of the value of the items you
are shipping.

Depending on the Project type with which you are working, the journal
entries associated with shipments and their invoices are different.
Some of the factors influencing these journal entries include:

* If the project has progress
  payments
* The type of contract, for
  example, cost types.
* If the project is recognizing
  sales based on a percentage of completion method.

VISUALs hierarchy for obtaining revenue and cost of goods sold
reflects common practices in the Project and Aerospace and Defense
industry. For example, establishing revenue and cost of goods sold
accounts by business line instead of the more commonly used product
code. This is important because companies in this industry may sell
the same products to several customers each of whom need to be associated
with different revenue accounts. You would normally associate a con
tract or project with a business linemany businesses establish "Profit
and Loss Centers" at the business line level to coincide with
their organizational management structures.

To accommodate businesses working in the standard manufacturing,
project/aerospace and defense, or mixed mode environments, VISUAL
allows the changing of G/L Account hierarchy. For example, if you
are working in the project environment, VISUALs uses a different
hierarchy of accounts than when you are working in a standard manufacturing
environment.
